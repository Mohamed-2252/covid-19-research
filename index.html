The mechanism of the disease:


COVID-19 is similar to the other viruses. Viruses are non-living organisms; however, they have their own genetic materials and program that are capable of redirecting the cell machinery to replicate and live inside the cell and also, they can transfer from one organism to another as transferring from animals such as cats and camels to human by host-switching, which appeared in many diseases such as influenza, smallpox, and HIV. Coronavirus is composed of RNA, wrapped with protein, a lipid membrane, and a spiky protein surface that help the virus to fuse with cell receptors.
COVID-19 invades the respiratory system especially the alveolar epithelial through ACE2 receptor, then it enters the cell as an uncoated virus with a positive RNA genome, then it uses the host’s ribosomes to translate it into proteins that are necessary for making RNA polymerase that will make new RNA negative strand and the negative strand will create a positive one, then the RNA strands will be read by the endoplasmic reticulum to make necessary components of the virus and send them to the Golgi apparatus to package them up with the positive strand and form new viruses, then they exit the cell by exocytosis after damaging the cell, after that the immune system invades those cells as a response for the cytokines secreted by the damaged cells, and the macrophages themselves secrete different types of cytokines, the inflammatory process, that occurs in the parenchyma in the alveoli, stimulates nerve endings causing the initiation of the reflex cough.

Symptoms of COVID-19:


The symptoms of coronaviruses vary from mild to severe symptoms since 2002, those symptoms usually appear after the incubation period of the disease which extends between 2-14 days, and they can be represented in fever or chills, dry cough, sore throat, muscle pain, headache, fatigue, loss of taste or smell, shortness and difficulty in breathing, but in severe conditions, the disease causes pneumonia.

Diagnosis of COVID-19
There are many tests used for the detection of coronavirus, however, the recommended and direct test to detect its presence is reverse transcription-polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR), which depends on the extraction of the viral RNA in swaps taken from the nose or the mouth by adding buffer, then convert the viral RNA to DNA by reverse transcription, after that multiple copies of the viral DNA are made, finally the sample is detected by real-time fluorescent PCR analyzer (RT-PCR instrument). There is another test which is an indirect test, that detects the presence of antibodies of coronavirus to know if the virus has infected the body or not, and it also determines the strength of your immune system and its defense, but this test isn’t always true and it has relatively low accuracy than the RT-PCR, which is very accurate, sensitive, but it takes a longer time.

COVID-19 impact on social life:


The recent Covid-19 pandemic has had significant psychological and social effects on the population. Research has highlighted the impact on the psychological well-being of the most exposed groups, including children, college students, and health workers, who are more likely to develop post-traumatic stress disorder, anxiety, depression, and other symptoms of distress. It has a drastic effect on people with special needs. Even at the best of times, persons with disabilities face challenges in accessing healthcare services, due to lack of availability, accessibility, affordability, as well as stigma and discrimination. The risks of infection from COVID-19 for persons with disabilities are compounded by other issues, which warrant specific action: disruption of services and support, pre-existing health conditions in some cases which leave them more at risk of developing serious illness or dying, being excluded from health information and mainstream health provision, living in a world where accessibility is often limited and where barriers to goods and services are a challenge, and being disproportionately more likely to live in institutional settings. General individual self-care and other preventive measures against the COVID-19 outbreak can entail challenges for persons with disabilities. For instance, some persons with disabilities may have difficulties in implementing measures to keep the virus at bay, including personal hygiene and recommended frequent cleaning of surfaces and homes. Cleaning homes and washing hands frequently can be challenging, due to physical impairments, environmental barriers, or interrupted services. Others may not be able to practice social distancing or cannot isolate themselves as thoroughly as other people, because they require regular help and support from other people for everyday self-care tasks. As the coronavirus pandemic rapidly sweeps across the world, it is inducing a considerable degree of fear, worry, and concern in the population at large and among certain groups in particular, such as older adults, care providers, and people with underlying health conditions.
In public mental health terms, the main psychological impact to date is elevated rates of stress or anxiety. But as new measures and impacts are introduced – especially quarantine and its effects on many people’s usual activities, routines, or livelihoods – levels of loneliness, depression, harmful alcohol, and drug use, and self-harm or suicidal behavior are also expected to rise.

The primary point of being in school is that it increases a child’s ability, social skills, and social awareness. But there is a good side of the impacts of COVID-19 on education: There are many online educational platforms that have been advertising-free educational tools for students around the world who are isolated at home due to COVID-19.

Positive environmental effects of covid-19
There is a lot of positive impacts that covid-19 has brought to the environment. Through satellite images published by NASA and the European Space Agency, a decrease in China's nitrogen dioxide emissions by nearly 30% compared to the previous five-year average., mostly from fossil fuel use, was observed in January and February. Also, the nitrogen dioxide levels fell by more than 30%. This resulted from the economic slowdown that this country faced, during the quarantine period. Also, Carbon dioxide emissions, that caused by the use of fossil fuels, also decreased, by 25 percent due to the measures taken to contain the pandemic outbreak of the Coronavirus. But, after the collapsing of the economy of some countries as a result of the complete closures during the Corona pandemic, some countries gradually reopened airports and restored the movement of transportation, which led to an increase in the proportion of nitrogen dioxide again, as shown in the first figure, after its emissions had decreased by a large percentage. In Italy, data from satellite imagery showed that nitrogen dioxide emissions have also decreased in the north of the country, and waterways in cities appear cleaner, due to the massive decrease in tourist boat traffic.
In the great lakes region, scientists observed that there is a slight increase in the rate of chlorophyll-a and suspended sediment concentrations in the Western Basin of Lake Erie, which considered the most affected lake part by human activities and agriculture. Research is ongoing, however, as scientists are unsure whether these changes are due to COVID-19 related behavioral changes or no.
In India, the curfew that was imposed across the country on the 22nd of March, caused the level of nitrogen dioxide pollution to reach its lowest levels during the spring.
Some animals have the opportunity to discover the world in the absence of humans. The lack of traffic on the streets saved the young animals, who began to wake up from their hibernation-like hedgehogs, from being run over under the wheels of cars.
In Egypt
It was confirmed by official government announcements that the environmental noise level in Egypt had decreased by about 75% during the lockdown period.
The lockdown period had affected the air pollution levels in Egypt with a focus on Cairo and Alexandria governorates. It was found that the AAI decreased by about 30%, the NO2 decreased by 15 and 33% over Cairo and Alexandria governorates, respectively, and that the CO decreased by about 5% over both governorates. In addition, the GHG emissions in Egypt were reduced by at least 4%. In contrast, ozone levels increased by about 2% over Cairo and Alexandria governorates.

Negative environmental effects of covid-19
Covid 19 has a negative impact on the environment. One of the negative effects of the pandemic is the significant increase in plastic waste, from medical gloves to packaging. The reason for this is due to people's trend towards packaged and prepackaged diets. Even the cafes that used to encourage customers to bring their cups in order to save on plastic cups that are harmful to the environment, stopped doing this for fear of spreading infection.
Biomedical waste (BMW) production from COVID-19 patients is increasing worldwide. India produces approximately 550 tons of biomedical waste (BMW) annually which is only treated by 198 joint biomedical waste treatment facilities and 225 captive incinerators. During the pandemic in Wuhan, China, it was reported that the city produced 240 tons of Additional medical waste per day.

Coronavirus effects on the economy:


The global economy was severely affected by the implementation of large-scale containment measures by governments to contain the spread of the COVID-19 virus. Many people have lost their jobs, or their incomes cut. Also, Unemployment rates have increased across major economies. The tourism and travel sector were the most affected among the economic sectors, due to the cessation of flights and the closing of many countries to their borders, especially in Asia, Europe, and the United States. Also, there have been major disruptions in other economic sectors, such as trade, energy, and finance. Global financial markets have been severely affected by the effects of the spread of COVID-19. With the number of cases increasing globally, mainly through the United States, Italy, Spain, Germany, France, Iran, and South Korea, the global financial and oil markets have collapsed since the beginning of 2020, So, the US stock market and European indices (S&P 500, FTSE 100, CAC 40, DAX) has been lost a quarter of its value, with oil prices down more than 65% as of April 24, 2020. coronavirus has unevenly impacted global food supply chains. In higher-income countries with more isolated agricultural communities and less intensive cultivation techniques, the virus's spread has had a minimal effect on key staple crops like wheat, maize, rice, and soybeans. Also, in some middle and lower-income countries where mandatory shelter-in-place orders have strained the cultivation of labor-intensive crops, the U.N. World Food Program estimated that the pandemic could double the number of people facing acute food shortages by the end of 2020.

Vaccinations:


There are 3 types of vaccinations The first one is the Messenger RNA vaccine. This vaccine uses genetically engineered mRNA to give your cells instructions for how to make the S protein found on the surface of the COVID-19 virus. After vaccination, your immune cells begin making the S protein pieces and displaying them on cell surfaces. This causes your body to create antibodies. If you later become infected with the COVID-19 virus, these antibodies will fight the virus. After delivering instructions, the mRNA is immediately broken down. It never enters the nucleus of your cells, where your DNA is kept. Both Pfizer-BioNTech and the Moderna use this method. The second one is the Vector vaccine. Genetic material from the COVID-19 virus is placed in a modified version of a different virus (viral vector). When the viral vector gets into your cells, it delivers genetic material from the COVID-19 virus that gives your cells instructions to make copies of the S protein. Once your cells display the S proteins on their surfaces, your immune system responds by creating antibodies and defensive white blood cells. If you later become infected with the COVID-19 virus, the antibodies will fight the virus. But do not worry, the genetic material that's delivered doesn't become part of your DNA. The Janssen/Johnson & Johnson COVID-19 vaccine is a vector vaccine. AstraZeneca and the University of Oxford also have a vector COVID-19 vaccine. The final one is the Protein subunit vaccine. Subunit vaccines include only the parts of a virus that best stimulate your immune system. This type of COVID-19 vaccine contains harmless S proteins. Once your immune system recognizes the S proteins, it creates antibodies and defensive white blood cells. If you later become infected with the COVID-19 virus, the antibodies will fight the virus. COVID-19 vaccination will help protect you from getting COVID-19. You may have some side effects, which are normal signs that your body is building protection. These side effects may affect your ability to do daily activities, but they should go away in a few days. Some people have no side effects.
